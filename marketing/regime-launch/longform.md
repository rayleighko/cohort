**"VOO and chill" isn't a strategy. It's a forecast you didn't know you were making.**

When you put everything in the S&P 500 and walk away, you think you're avoiding a
market call. You're not. You're making the most aggressive one there is: that US
large-cap growth keeps winning, that inflation stays tame, and that the regime
that's been good to you since 2009 simply doesn't break.

Most of the time, that bet pays. That's the trap. It pays right up until the one
environment it can't survive.

2022 was that environment. The S&P fell about 18% — its worst year since 2008.
Stocks fall, fine. But here's the part nobody priced in: bonds fell with them. The
10-year Treasury lost 17.8% — the worst year for US bonds on record. Stocks and
bonds dropped almost in lockstep. The thing that's supposed to catch you didn't.

Now rewind to 2008. Stocks cratered about 37%, far worse than 2022. Yet a 60/40
portfolio held up better — because the 10-year Treasury gained 20.1% and cushioned
the fall. Same kind of crash, opposite outcome. 2008 was a growth scare. 2022 was an
inflation shock. Your portfolio cares, deeply, which one shows up.

And no — diversification isn't a magic word either. A classic All Weather mix had a
rough 2022 too. When cash is the only thing rising, almost nothing is spared. Anyone
selling you a portfolio that "wins in every regime" is selling you the 2009–2021
backtest and hoping you don't ask about the rest.

So the real question was never "is my portfolio good." It's: which economic regime
is my portfolio secretly betting on — and which one quietly breaks it?

stored. → thebearings.app
